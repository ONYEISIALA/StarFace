🎮 Game Modes

Survival Mode

Players must gather resources (wood, stone, food, etc.), craft tools, and build shelters.

Health and hunger must be managed.

Enemies like zombies, skeletons, and creepers appear, especially at night or in dark areas.

Creative Mode

Unlimited resources.

Ability to fly.

No health or hunger, making it ideal for building and experimenting freely.

Adventure Mode

Designed for custom maps and adventures.

Players interact with objects (like switches and buttons) but can’t break blocks without proper tools.

Spectator Mode

Players can fly through blocks and watch the world without directly interacting.

🌍 World Features

Biomes: Different environments like forests, deserts, oceans, jungles, and snowy tundras.

Structures: Villages, temples, dungeons, mineshafts, strongholds, and more.

The Nether: A fiery, dangerous dimension with unique mobs and resources.

The End: A mysterious dimension where the Ender Dragon boss lives.

🛠️ Core Gameplay Mechanics

Crafting System: Combine resources to make tools, weapons, armor, and machines.

Mining: Dig through layers of earth and stone to find ores like coal, iron, gold, diamond, and even Netherite.

Building: Place blocks to create houses, castles, farms, or entire cities.

Redstone: A powerful material used to build circuits, machines, traps, and even computers inside Minecraft.

🤝 Multiplayer

Players can join servers to play together.

Mini-games like SkyWars, BedWars, and Hunger Games exist on popular servers.

Realms: Private servers hosted by Mojang for friends and families.

🐾 Mobs (Creatures)

Passive mobs: Cows, pigs, sheep, chickens (useful for food and resources).

Neutral mobs: Wolves, Endermen, bees (attack if provoked).

Hostile mobs: Creepers, zombies, skeletons, spiders, Ender Dragon, Wither.

🎨 Customization

Skins: Players can change how their character looks.

Mods & Add-ons: Extend the game with new content, mechanics, and features.

Texture Packs: Change the visual appearance of blocks and items.

📱 Platforms

Available on PC, consoles (Xbox, PlayStation, Switch), and mobile devices.

Cross-play is supported between many platforms.
